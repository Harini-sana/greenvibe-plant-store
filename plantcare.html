💧 Watering Your Plants

💧 Check Soil Moisture

Stick your finger 1-2 inches into the soil. If it's dry, it's time to water. Most houseplants prefer slightly moist soil.

🕐 Best Time to Water

Water plants in the morning so they can absorb moisture throughout the day and dry before evening.

🚿 Watering Technique

Water slowly and thoroughly until water drains from the bottom. Avoid getting water on leaves.

💧 Water Quality

Use room temperature water. If possible, let tap water sit overnight to allow chlorine to evaporate.

☀️ Light Requirements

🌞 Bright Direct Light

Plants that need 6+ hours of direct sunlight. Place near south-facing windows. Examples: Succulents, Cacti.

🌤️ Bright Indirect Light

Bright light without direct sun rays. Perfect for most houseplants. Examples: Pothos, Snake Plants.

🌫️ Medium Light

Areas with some natural light but not too bright. Examples: Peace Lily, ZZ Plant.

🌑 Low Light

Areas with minimal natural light. Few plants thrive here. Examples: Cast Iron Plant, Chinese Evergreen.

🌱 Soil & Fertilizer

🏡 Potting Mix

Use well-draining potting soil. Never use garden soil for containers as it's too heavy and doesn't drain well.

🌵 Specialized Soils

Succulents need cactus mix, orchids need orchid bark mix. Match the soil to your plant's needs.

🍽️ Fertilizing Schedule

Feed most houseplants monthly during spring/summer with diluted liquid fertilizer. Reduce in fall/winter.

🔄 Repotting

Repot when roots are coming out of drainage holes or soil stays wet too long. Usually every 1-2 years.

🐛 Common Problems

🟡 Yellow Leaves

Usually overwatering or natural aging. Check soil moisture and adjust watering schedule.

🟤 Brown Leaf Tips

Low humidity, fluoride in water, or over-fertilizing. Increase humidity and use filtered water.

🐛 Pests

Common pests: spider mites, aphids, mealybugs. Use insecticidal soap or neem oil treatment.

🍄 Fungal Issues

Often from overwatering. Improve air circulation, reduce watering, remove affected parts.

🍂 Seasonal Care

🌸 Spring Care

Increase watering and feeding. Perfect time for repotting and propagating as plants start active growth.

☀️ Summer Care

Water more frequently, provide shade from intense sun, watch for pests, and ensure good air circulation.

🍂 Fall Care

Gradually reduce watering and stop fertilizing. Bring outdoor plants inside before temperature drops.

❄️ Winter Care

Water less frequently, increase humidity, provide extra light, and avoid cold drafts.

🌿 Plant Propagation

✂️ Stem Cuttings

Cut 4-6 inch stems below a node. Root in water or soil. Works for pothos, philodendrons, and many herbs.

🍃 Leaf Cuttings

Carefully remove whole leaves and place on soil. Perfect for succulents like jade plants and echeveria.

🌱 Division

Separate root systems of mature plants. Works well for snake plants, peace lilies, and spider plants.

🌰 Seeds

Start seeds in seed-starting mix, provide warmth and consistent moisture. Be patient - germination takes time.
